Photosynthesis is the process plants use to convert light energy into chemical energy stored in glucose. Most photosynthesis happens in leaf cells, especially in chloroplasts that contain chlorophyll, a green pigment that captures light.

In the light-dependent reactions, plants use sunlight, water, and chlorophyll to produce energy carriers. Water molecules are split, oxygen is released, and energy is captured to power later steps. This is why plants help replenish oxygen in Earth's atmosphere.

In the Calvin cycle, the plant uses carbon dioxide from the air together with energy from the light reactions to build sugars. Those sugars support plant growth and can later be used as food energy by animals and humans through food chains.
